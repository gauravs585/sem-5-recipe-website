Samosa

Ingredients:

For the Samosa Filling:

- 2 cups of boiled and mashed potatoes

- 1/2 cup of boiled and chopped mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, and green beans)

- 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds

- 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste

- 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder

- 1 teaspoon of coriander powder

- 1 teaspoon of garam masala

- Salt to taste

- 2 tablespoons of oil

For the Samosa Dough:

- 2 cups of all-purpose flour

- 1/4 cup of oil or ghee

- Water for kneading

- Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. Start by making the samosa dough. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and oil (or ghee). Mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Gradually add water and knead it into a smooth, stiff dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

2. While the dough is resting, prepare the samosa filling. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.

3. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

4. Add turmeric, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir well.

5. Add the boiled and mashed potatoes and mixed vegetables. Mix everything together and cook for a few minutes. Season with salt to taste. The filling should be well-spiced and dry. Set it aside to cool.

6. Divide the rested dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Roll out each ball into a thin oval or circle.

7. Cut the rolled-out dough in half to form two semi-circles.

8. Take one semi-circle, fold it in half to form a cone shape, and seal the edges with a little water.

9. Fill the cone with the prepared potato and vegetable mixture.

10. Seal the open edge of the cone to form a triangular samosa. Press the edges firmly to ensure they are sealed properly.

11. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. To check if the oil is ready for frying, drop a small piece of dough into it; it should sizzle and rise to the surface.

12. Carefully slide the samosas into the hot oil and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy.

13. Remove the fried samosas and drain them on a paper towel to remove excess oil.

Your delicious homemade samosas are ready to be served. Enjoy them with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.
